# Quest Examples — LLM Generated (April 2026)

These quests were generated by an external LLM to stress-test the knowledge/gossip system and
inform the design of `QuestSystem`. They are source material for system requirements, not final
game content. Two separate runs are preserved below.

---

## Run A — Six Quests (GPT-style, narrative-heavy)

### Quest A1: The Bait and the Bloodhound

**Premise:** A heavily laden, battle-damaged Spanish treasure galleon is reportedly limping alone through the Florida Straits, separated from its fleet after a storm.

**Knowledge trigger:** Ship location (`Spanish Galleon San Jose` in the Florida Straits) at **High** confidence.

**Hook:** Emerges from a high-confidence rumor network after the player visits a Pirate Brethren haven, where a surviving scout ship brought back the tale.

**Player choices:**
- **Attack the Galleon:** The player sails to the straits and intercepts the ship.
  - *New facts:* "Spanish deploying Q-ships in Florida Straits" (High confidence to Pirate Brethren/Independents).
  - *Superseded:* "Spanish Galleon San Jose is vulnerable" becomes immediately false.
- **Sell the Coordinates:** The player sells the ship's location to a rival pirate captain in port.
  - *New facts:* "Captain Vane was sunk by the Spanish" (Medium confidence across all factions).
  - *Superseded:* "Captain Vane's ship location" is wiped.

**Failure / expiry:** If ignored for a week, the ship reaches port. Location fact decays to 0, window closes.

**Disinformation angle (central mechanic):** The Spanish intentionally planted the "vulnerable galleon" rumour among pirate ports to lure them out. Acting on the High-confidence rumour without scouting → devastating ambush.

### Quest A2: A Squeeze on Sweetness

**Premise:** The Dutch Traders Guild is quietly buying up all sugar in the Lesser Antilles and hoarding it in secret caches to manufacture an artificial shortage.

**Knowledge trigger:** Price anomaly (Sugar prices rocketing in English ports, plummeting in Dutch ports) at **Medium** confidence.

**Hook:** A desperate, bankrupt independent merchant in a tavern connects the dots on the strange pricing data.

**Player choices:**
- **Smuggle for the Starving:** Buy cheap Dutch sugar, sell to desperate English ports.
  - *New facts:* "Player is enemy of Dutch Guild" (High confidence to Dutch). "Sugar prices normalizing in English ports" (High confidence to English).
  - *Superseded:* Original price anomaly facts overwritten.
- **Join the Syndicate:** Protect Dutch hoarding ships from pirates.
  - *New facts:* "Dutch hold absolute sugar monopoly" (High confidence across Caribbean). "Player is ally of Dutch" (High confidence to Dutch).
  - *Superseded:* "Dutch port sugar prices plummeting" becomes false.

**Failure / expiry:** Dutch consolidate monopoly; price anomaly becomes new baseline; arbitrage opportunity vanishes.

**Disinformation angle:** Dutch spread rumours of a "blight on the sugar crop" to hide the hoarding.

---

### Quest A3: The Governor's Quill

**Premise:** The French governor in Martinique is secretly corresponding with the English Crown to negotiate his defection and the surrender of the port.

**Knowledge trigger:** Named individual status (`Governor Dubois` loyalty wavering) at **Low** confidence.

**Hook:** An intercepted courier ship yields a locked chest with partially coded, vaguely treasonous letters.

**Player choices:**
- **Deliver to French Inquisition:**
  - *New facts:* "Governor Dubois executed" (High confidence to French). "Martinique under martial law" (High confidence to all nearby ships).
  - *Superseded:* Dubois' status → dead/deposed.
- **Sell to English:**
  - *New facts:* "England preparing invasion fleet for Martinique" (Low confidence, spreading slowly from English ports).
  - *Superseded:* Dubois' loyalty now aligned with English (known only to English/Player).
- **Blackmail the Governor:**
  - *New facts:* "Governor Dubois fiercely loyal to France" (High confidence, spread intentionally by Dubois to cover tracks).
  - *Superseded:* Original low-confidence defection rumour actively scrubbed by French agents.

**Failure / expiry:** If player doesn't act before English fleet arrives, defection happens automatically.

**Disinformation angle:** None. Raw political intelligence.

### Quest A4: The Rot in the Timbers

**Premise:** Yellow fever has decimated the English garrison at Port Royal, leaving coastal forts unmanned.

**Knowledge trigger:** Faction strength (`English military strength at Port Royal` = critical) at **High** confidence.

**Hook:** Overheard from a delirious, infected English deserter begging for water in a neutral port.

**Player choices:**
- **Lead a Pirate Raid:**
  - *New facts:* "Port Royal sacked by pirates" (High confidence across map). "English Crown heavily weakened" (Medium confidence everywhere).
  - *Superseded:* "Port Royal is highly defended" permanently shattered.
- **Extort with Medicine:**
  - *New facts:* "Port Royal garrison recovering" (Medium confidence to English). "Player is a gouging opportunist" (Low confidence among merchants).
  - *Superseded:* "Port Royal strength critical" becomes false.

**Failure / expiry (confidence decay as core mechanic):** The High-confidence fact decays as the player travels. If they arrive after decay, a reinforcement fleet has already arrived — the "empty fort" fact is fatally false.

**Disinformation angle:** Spanish could plant this to goad pirates into weakening English defences before a Spanish armada arrives.

### Quest A5: The Eye of the Tempest

**Premise:** A massive hurricane's calm eye is passing directly over an uncharted reef where a legendary smuggler's stash rests.

**Knowledge trigger:** Weather (Hurricane path over `Serpent's Reef`) at **Medium** confidence.

**Hook:** Player observes strange barometric cloud formations matching an old navigator's warning about the "Serpent's Breath."

**Player choices:**
- **Brave the Storm:** Sail into hurricane (ship damage) to reach the eye and loot the stash.
  - *New facts:* "Smuggler's stash at Serpent's Reef is empty" (High confidence to player, slowly leaking to Independents).
  - *Superseded:* All rumours of the "Lost Stash" permanently false.
- **Sell the Pathing:** Sell hurricane path data to Dutch Traders Guild for safe convoy routing.
  - *New facts:* "Dutch convoys survived the Great Tempest" (High confidence everywhere).
  - *Superseded:* Current weather patterns updated for Dutch faction.

**Failure / expiry:** Hurricane moves in 3 in-game days; weather fact supersedes itself; stash lost under waves.

**Disinformation angle:** A rival navigator could lie about the storm's path, sending the player into the deadly eyewall instead of the calm centre.

### Quest A6: The Bloodless Coup

**Premise:** The Pirate Brethren are quietly losing control of Nassau to a cartel of independent merchants who have bought off the harbour master and local guards.

**Knowledge trigger:** Port control (`Nassau control contested by merchants`) at **Low** confidence.

**Hook:** Gossip at a Spanish port where trade tariffs on Nassau-origin goods have mysteriously vanished, indicating a regime change.

**Player choices:**
- **Back the Pirates:** Assassinate the corrupt harbour master to restore Pirate Brethren control.
  - *New facts:* "Nassau firmly under Pirate control" (High confidence to Pirates). "Merchant cartel leaders fleeing" (Medium confidence to Independents).
  - *Superseded:* "Nassau contested" rumour crushed.
- **Back the Merchants:** Smuggle weapons into Nassau to help merchants formalise the takeover.
  - *New facts:* "Nassau is an Independent Port" (High confidence across map). "Pirate Brethren expelled from Nassau" (High confidence everywhere).
  - *Superseded:* Long-standing "Nassau is a Pirate port" becomes false.

**Failure / expiry:** Merchants eventually win the economic war. Port control fact slowly updates across the map from Pirate → Independent as merchant ships spread the word.

**Disinformation angle:** Spanish spread the "contested" rumour to make Nassau look vulnerable, hoping English or French waste a fleet attacking it while pirates are supposedly distracted.

## Run B — Six Quests (tighter scope, system-focused)

### Quest B1: The Governor's Shadow

**Premise:** A supposedly Spanish-controlled port is quietly being negotiated away to the English—but only insiders know, and the timeline is unclear.

**Knowledge trigger:**
- Port control: "Port San Marcos controlled by Spanish Empire" (High confidence)
- Named individual status: "Governor Esteban Ruiz in secret talks with English agents" (Low–Medium confidence)

**Hook:** An anxious clerk approaches the player at port after overhearing them repeat the Ruiz rumour.

**Player choices:**
1. **Expose to Spanish authorities** — gather proof, intercept letters.
   - *New facts:* "Governor Ruiz arrested for treason" (High local, Medium elsewhere). "Spanish tighten control over San Marcos" (Medium).
   - *Superseded:* "Ruiz negotiating with English" obsolete.
2. **Assist English takeover** — ferry messages, block Spanish patrols.
   - *New facts:* "San Marcos now controlled by English Crown" (Medium, spreads). "Spanish fleet caught off guard" (Low rumour).
   - *Superseded:* Spanish control claim becomes false.
3. **Blackmail Ruiz for personal gain.**
   - *New facts:* "Governor Ruiz compromised" (Low confidence, spreads as gossip).
   - *Superseded:* None immediately; instability increases future volatility.

**Failure / expiry:** Negotiation resolves offscreen — control flips based on hidden faction strength. Player's knowledge becomes incorrect.

**Disinformation angle:** English may have planted the "secret talks" rumour to destabilise Spanish loyalty. Acting on false info → arrest or ambush.

### Quest B2: The Sugar Windfall

**Premise:** A sudden sugar shortage has driven prices sky-high in one port—but the cause may already be resolving.

**Knowledge trigger:** Price anomaly: "Sugar prices in Port-au-Prince extremely high" (Medium–High confidence).

**Hook:** A Dutch trader excitedly shares price sheets in a tavern.

**Player choices:**
1. **Exploit the trade window** — buy sugar elsewhere, rush to port.
   - *New facts:* "Prices stabilizing in Port-au-Prince" (Medium).
   - *Superseded:* High-price claim outdated quickly.
2. **Investigate the cause** — discover delayed convoy or storm damage.
   - *New facts:* "Supply disruption due to storm damage" (High local). "Convoy expected soon" (Medium).
   - *Superseded:* "Persistent shortage" becomes false.
3. **Sabotage incoming supply** — attack convoy to prolong scarcity.
   - *New facts:* "Convoy lost at sea" (Low–Medium, may be misattributed).
   - *Superseded:* "Supply arriving soon" becomes false.

**Failure / expiry:** Prices normalise as supply arrives; confidence in anomaly decays rapidly, closing the window.

### Quest B3: The Vanishing Frigate

**Premise:** A heavily armed English frigate has disappeared, and rumours conflict — sunk, defected, or hiding?

**Knowledge trigger:**
- Ship location: "HMS Resolute last seen near Windward Passage" (Medium confidence)
- Faction strength: "English naval presence weakened in region" (Low confidence)

**Hook:** Spotted drifting wreckage, or conflicting tavern accounts across multiple ports.

**Player choices:**
1. **Track and rescue survivors.**
   - *New facts:* "Resolute sunk in storm" (High if confirmed). "English fleet reduced" (Medium).
   - *Superseded:* Defection/piracy rumours become false.
2. **Hunt as a prize** — board or capture if alive.
   - *New facts:* "Resolute captured" (Medium, spreading).
   - *Superseded:* "Ship missing" replaced with new ownership.
3. **Sell false intel** — spread claim it defected to pirates.
   - *New facts:* "Resolute joined Pirate Brethren" (Low but spreads fast).
   - *Superseded:* Creates competing narratives; no clear truth.

**Failure / expiry:** Wreck decays, survivors die, or ship reappears — ambiguity resolves without player.

### Quest B4: A Cure in Doubt

**Premise:** A plague is reported in a French port, but no one agrees whether it's real or exaggerated.

**Knowledge trigger:**
- Named individual status: "Doctor Émile Laurent claims outbreak in Saint-Pierre" (Low confidence)
- Port condition (implicit): disease presence uncertain

**Hook:** A desperate sailor begs passage for medicine, citing the doctor's warnings.

**Player choices:**
1. **Deliver medicine** — risk entering port.
   - *New facts:* "Plague confirmed in Saint-Pierre" OR "False alarm" (High confidence depending on truth).
   - *Superseded:* Uncertainty resolved.
2. **Exploit panic** — buy goods cheap, resell elsewhere.
   - *New facts:* "Saint-Pierre economy unstable" (Medium).
3. **Verify independently** — interrogate ships, observe conditions.
   - *New facts:* High-confidence confirmation or debunk.
   - *Superseded:* Doctor's claim validated or discredited.

**Failure / expiry:** Outbreak either spreads (becomes obvious) or fades (rumour collapses). Timing determines truth.

### Quest B5: The Silent Convoy

**Premise:** A Dutch convoy is rumoured to be travelling without escort, carrying valuable spices — but sightings conflict.

**Knowledge trigger:** Ship location: "Dutch spice convoy sailing unguarded near Curaçao" (Low–Medium confidence).

**Hook:** Fragmented reports from multiple sailors, each slightly different.

**Player choices:**
1. **Ambush the convoy.**
   - *New facts:* "Convoy attacked/lost" (Medium spreading).
   - *Superseded:* "Convoy en route safely."
2. **Escort for the Dutch.**
   - *New facts:* "Convoy arrived safely under escort" (Medium).
   - *Superseded:* "Unguarded convoy" becomes false.
3. **Ignore and trade elsewhere** — no player-created facts.

**Failure / expiry:** Convoy reaches destination or is attacked by others; rumour resolves into history.

**Disinformation angle (central mechanic):** Dutch deliberately spread "unguarded" rumours to lure pirates into ambush by hidden escorts.

### Quest B6: The Map That Wasn't

**Premise:** A pirate claims to possess a map to a hidden safe harbour—but authenticity is questionable.

**Knowledge trigger:**
- Named individual status: "Captain Inez 'Redwake' Vargas possesses a map to a hidden port" (Low confidence)
- Weather (optional): "Storms obscure certain sea lanes" (Medium confidence)

**Hook:** The pirate approaches the player directly, offering to sell or trade the map.

**Player choices:**
1. **Buy and verify** — sail to location.
   - *New facts:* "Hidden harbour exists at [location]" OR "Map is fake" (High after verification).
   - *Superseded:* Pirate's claim confirmed or invalidated.
2. **Sell the rumour onward** — spread without verifying.
   - *New facts:* "Hidden harbour rumoured among pirates" (Low, spreads widely).
   - *Superseded:* None; ambiguity increases.
3. **Turn pirate in to authorities.**
   - *New facts:* "Redwake captured" (Medium). Map possibly lost/seized (Low).
   - *Superseded:* Map claim harder to verify.

**Failure / expiry:** Another captain verifies or disproves the map; knowledge becomes common and loses value.

**Disinformation angle (central mechanic):** Map may be intentionally fake (lure rivals onto reefs) or partially correct (location real, route wrong). Acting on false belief can destroy the player's ship — or create a persistent false rumour in the world.
